# Project Overview

This collaborative NSF-DST project aims to advance next-generation optical communication systems by integrating physical-layer models with intelligent network-layer algorithms for multi-core coherent optical WDM networks.

The project is jointly conducted by the Networks Research Group at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln (UNL) and collaborators at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras (IITM). Through international collaboration, testbed development, student exchanges, and joint research activities, the project seeks to improve the performance, scalability, and reliability of future optical communication networks.

---

# Research Objectives

The primary objectives of this project include:

- Optical Phase Conjugation for signal quality enhancement.
- Space Division Multiplexing (SDM) using multi-core fibers.
- Intelligent resource allocation algorithms.
- Integration of physical-layer impairments into network simulations.
- Experimental validation using optical networking testbeds.
